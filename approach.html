Our Approach

We recognize that your story is so much more than a diagnosis stage and a treatment plan, and everyone’s journey through cancer looks entirely different. This is why we take pride in learning about you and your story. Specifically, our innovative machine learning algorithm is trained to analyze factors such as speaking language, parenting situations, gender, personal concerns (financial, emotional, etc.), and more.

Our Services

OncoSupport offers a variety of services to assist you with your cancer journey. Below are the key services we offer to our patients and caregivers:

Support Groups

Our support group initiatives focus on creating a nurturing environment where you can freely express your concerns, access resources, and receive encouragement, ultimately promoting healing and resilience in the face of cancer’s hardships. Unlike many local oncology therapy centers, our algorithm specializes in connecting you with individuals going through a similar/same treatment experience. Here’s the strategy of our algorithm at a glance:

  • Cancer Diagnosis (see www.cancer.gov/types for your diagnosis)
  • Cancer Stage (0, I, II, III, IV; see your oncologist to learn your diagnosis stage)
  • Time spent with cancer (months)
  • Age
  • Gender
  • Primary Speaking Language
  • Parenting Situation
  • Specific concerns regarding cancer (financial concerns, emotional struggles, lack of knowledge, etc.)

Educational Events

We believe that you have the right to have as much information available to make the best decision for you. That’s why we have partnered with the leading oncologists in the nation to host educational seminars that are designed for you to learn more about your cancer. Here are a few topics that are covered at our seminars:

  • Necessary information regarding a specific diagnosis (Leukemia, Breast Cancer, Lung Cancer, etc.)
  • Treatment options for different cancers
  • Dietary restrictions for cancer patients

Personalized Consultation

As a community, we want to know how we are helping you in your cancer journey, and what we can do better. This is why our Patient Wellness team is dedicated to meeting with you once a month, or more if necessary, to offer our own support and to improve our personal services towards you. Below is a few examples of what our volunteers would discuss with you:

  • How you’re fitting in with your support group
  • Any educational seminars you would like to hear/be apart of
  • Patients that you would like to be connected with (Ex. a patient that has experiences a specific procedure, a patient with a child, etc.)

Personal Communication

Not only do we provide you with individuals to connect with, but we also give you an entire platform to chat and engage with your fellow patients. After you complete the intake form, follow the directions on the resulting page to meet your support group on our chat application, which allows you to conduct one-on-one chats, group chats, and set up meetings with Google Meet.

Having access to personal connections is especially helpful for patients seeking advice or insight on a particular procedure that another patient has received. This not only puts you at ease since you have more information available to you, but it improves your cancer experience overall. Cancer can be incredibly isolating, and having a support group who is also going through a similar journey is greatly comforting.
